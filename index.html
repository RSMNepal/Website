Who We Are

Established in 2080 B.S., Rastriya Shramik Mahasangh Nepal, originally founded as Rastriya Krishi Shramik Sangh Nepal in 2068 B.S., is the only independent organization dedicated to safeguarding the rights and interests of the working class and uplifting their living standards for overall national development.

Our organization comprises 11 unions and 2 industrial units, with provincial committees and district committees in all provinces of Nepal. We are committed to the industrialization of the nation, labor-oriented industries, and the nationalization of workers.

We stand firmly for protecting Nepal's soil, rich diversity, mesmerizing cultural heritage, and national identity, operating free from the influence of government, business owners, political parties, foreign ideologies, or personal leadership ambitions.

Our Journey

2067 B.S.

Established as Rastriya Krishi Shramik Sangh with a mission to safeguard worker welfare

Poush 2077 B.S.

Led protests of sugarcane farmers and successfully pressured sugar mill owners to pay outstanding dues

2080 B.S.

Establishment of Rastriya Shramik Mahasangh Nepal as an independent organization

2080 B.S.

Protected rights of industrial workers at Varun Beverages (PepsiCola) and Mount Everest Brewery

2081 B.S.

Submitted eight-point demand memorandum to Nepal government highlighting problems faced by farmers and agricultural workers

2081 B.S.

Implementation of employment programs across different districts to uplift living standards of informal sector workers

Present

Continuing to fight for labor rights, enhancing employment opportunities, and ensuring social security for all workers
