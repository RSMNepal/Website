Our Story

Nepal, an ancient and sacred land steeped in wisdom and tradition, has long been guided by the teachings of sages and monks who imparted not only material knowledge but also profound moral and ethical principles. Throughout history, structured frameworks have governed the delicate balance between the presiding class and laborers, establishing regulations and ethical norms to foster harmony and progress.

"Nationalise the Labour, Labourise the Industries, Industrialise the Nation"

In contemporary Nepal, our constitution, aligned with trade union laws, enshrines provisions for the protection and holistic development of workers' rights. Workers form the backbone of nation-building; no country can advance without their unwavering dedication and contributions. Yet, despite the proliferation of labor organizations, many remain entangled in political affiliations, often manipulated for partisan gains. This exploitation has stymied the success of trade union movements, reducing workers to mere vote banks and perpetuating their deprivation of essential rights and benefits.

Amid this challenging landscape, originally founded as Rastriya Krishi Shramik Sangh Nepal on Chaitra 11, 2068, evolved on Baishak 11, 2080 into Rastriya Shramik Mahasangh Nepal, as an independent beacon of hope dedicated to safeguarding Nepal's sovereign soil, its rich cultural diversity, mesmerizing heritage, and national identity while elevating the living standards of the working class for comprehensive national development. We stand as an independent force, safeguarding Nepal's sovereignty, cultural heritage, and national identity while elevating workers' living standards for national progress.

Committed to industrializing the nation, creating labor-oriented industries, and nationalizing the workforce, our principles prioritize national interest, industrial development, and labor rights. Operated by workers for workers, we remain free from government, business, political, foreign, or personal influences, embodying true autonomy in labor advocacy.

Phases of Struggle

Poush 2077 B.S.

Led pivotal protests for sugarcane farmers, compelling sugar mill owners to settle outstanding dues worth crores, addressing long-standing grievances in the agricultural sector.

Ongoing Industrial Advocacy

Actively protecting rights in key industries like Varun Beverages (PepsiCola) and Mount Everest Brewery, championing fair wages, job security, and improved working conditions through sustained movements.

2081 B.S.

Submitted an eight-point demand memorandum to the Nepal government via Chief District Officers, spotlighting challenges faced by farmers and agricultural workers in our agriculture-dominant nation, urging systemic resolutions.

Present Day

Continuing to spearhead labor movements, implement district-level employment programs for informal sector workers, and ensure social security while fighting for comprehensive worker prosperity.

We invite all workers and laborers to unite in rebuilding our nation, championing prosperity, and advancing workers' rights and well-being.

Organizational Structure

The Rastriya Shramik Mahasangh Nepal is a robust federation encompassing 11 specialized unions and 2 industrial units, with provincial and district committees established across all provinces of Nepal. Each affiliated union maintains its own provincial and district committees, ensuring grassroots representation and effective advocacy for workers nationwide.

Central Executive Committee

The Central Executive Committee (Kendriya Karya Samiti) serves as the pinnacle of decision-making within Rastriya Shramik Mahasangh Nepal, comprising visionary leaders who steer our mission with unwavering dedication to workers' empowerment.

Our Affiliated Unions

Rastriya Shramik Mahasangh Nepal unites 11 specialized unions, each championing workers in distinct sectors. Explore their missions and leadership.

Rastriya Krishi Shramik Sangh Nepal

Championing agricultural workers and farmers for fair wages and sustainable practices.

Rastriya Nai Shramik Sangh Nepal

Advocating for barbers and hairdressers' professional rights and development.

Rastriya Rangrohan Shramik Sangh Nepal

Protecting painters and dyeing workers with focus on safety and fair pay.

Rastriya Kastha Shramik Sangh Nepal

Supporting carpenters and woodworkers in skill enhancement and rights protection.

Rastriya Plumber Shramik Sangh Nepal

Ensuring plumbers' fair wages and safe working environments.

Rastriya Ghar Grihsti Shramik Sangh Nepal

Upholding dignity and rights for domestic workers.

Rastriya Nirman Shramik Sangh Nepal

Advocating safety and compensation for construction workers.

Rastriya Auto Rickshaw Chalak Shramik Sangh Nepal

Fighting for fair fares and conditions for auto rickshaw drivers.

Rastriya Kabadi Shramik Sangh Nepal

Promoting dignified work for waste pickers and recyclers.

Supporting brick kiln workers in fair practices.

Nepal Rastriya Shikshak Sangh
